# Product, Business, and Usability Excellence Policy

Generated: 2026-07-07
Purpose: Ensure the app becomes not merely functional, but commercially powerful, easy to manage, and useful for Oasis Baklawa operations.

---

# 1. Product Philosophy

The Oasis system must behave like an enterprise operating system for the business:

- capture demand
- reduce manual mistakes
- speed up orders
- protect governance
- increase sales visibility
- improve product/catalogue quality
- make management control easier
- make staff execution simpler

Every feature must answer: how does this help sales, operations, control, compliance, or management?

---

# 2. User Groups to Serve

Design and build for these users:

- owner/CMD
- admin/super admin
- sales team
- WhatsApp operator
- catalogue/product manager
- finance/accounts
- dispatch team
- warehouse team
- gatekeeper
- label/packing team
- distributor/super-stockist future workflows
- audit/compliance reviewer

Do not design only for developers.

---

# 3. Usability Standards

Important workflows must be:

- searchable
- filterable
- explainable
- role-aware
- fast to operate
- low-click for repeated tasks
- safe against accidental mutation
- clear about next action
- clear about blocker reason
- clear about who last acted

Operators should not need to understand database internals.

---

# 4. Business-Generation Features to Prioritize

High-value future features:

1. WhatsApp lead/order capture and follow-up
2. Fast product/SKU identification
3. Product recommendations/alternatives
4. Distributor/customer intelligence
5. Sales order draft to controlled Golden Pipeline promotion
6. Automated but gated quotation/order creation
7. Follow-up reminders for unresolved leads
8. Sales dashboards by customer/product/channel
9. Product media/catalogue generation governance
10. Stock/dispatch promise reliability

These should be built with governance, not shortcuts.

---

# 5. Management Control Features to Prioritize

Important management capabilities:

- live order status
- pending approval queues
- unresolved WhatsApp messages
- draft ageing
- product mismatch/low-confidence resolver queue
- finance blockers
- dispatch blockers
- stock readiness
- customer-wise performance
- product-wise performance
- user action audit
- exception reports

The owner should be able to see what is stuck, why it is stuck, who owns it, and what action is required.

---

# 6. AI Feature Governance

AI should assist, not silently control business truth.

AI may:

- suggest SKU/product matches
- draft product descriptions
- prepare media variations
- propose aliases
- summarize customer messages
- identify missing order information

AI must not automatically:

- create final product truth
- change SKU structure
- approve financial documents
- move stock
- dispatch goods
- override GST/HSN classification
- approve credit/payment terms
- delete audit history

---

# 7. Ease and Versatility Standards

The app should support:

- multiple product types
- loose/bulk and gift pack formats
- domestic and export packaging
- frozen and ambient workflows
- multiple sales channels
- manual override with audit
- future integrations
- role-specific dashboards
- configurable thresholds where useful

Versatility must come from well-modeled data, not from fragile hardcoding.

---

# 8. Avoid Feature Bloat

Do not build features merely because they sound advanced.

A feature should be delayed if:

- source of truth is unclear
- owner role is unclear
- validation path is unclear
- business value is weak
- it risks protected flows
- it creates maintenance burden without immediate payoff

Build depth before breadth.

---

# 9. Operator-Centric Standard

For high-frequency staff actions, the system must reduce friction:

- fewer clicks
- obvious next action
- keyboard/search support where useful
- clear alternatives
- visible confidence/reason
- easy correction
- no hidden save state
- no duplicate submissions

A smart system that staff avoids using is a failed system.

---

# 10. Owner-Centric Standard

For leadership views, the system must prioritize:

- exceptions
- pending money/order/dispatch blockers
- trends
- leakage/missed opportunities
- accountability
- time ageing
- commercial impact

Do not flood owner dashboards with raw operational noise.
